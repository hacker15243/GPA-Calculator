FAQ

What is unweighted GPA and how is it calculated?

Unweighted GPA is your Grade Point average solely based on the grades your receive in your classes. Each letter grade has a numerical value relating to it. The values for each grade are added up and divided by the total number of classes, forming your unweighted GPA.

What is weighted GPA and how is it calculated?

Your Weighted GPA takes into account the level of the classes you are taking. AP and Honors Classes boost your weighted GPA. It is calculated by taking the unweighted GPA, multiplying it by the number of classes, adding 1.0 for each AP or Honors course taken, and dividing that total by the class count.

How to use the GPA Calculator

On the home page, sign in if you wish for your data to be saved for the next time you visit this website. Then, click the calculator tab at the navigation bar on top to get started! Click the add course button to add your classes one by one. Enter the letter grade and course type for each one. Once you're done, simply click the 'Calculate' Button and you will get your weighted and unweighted GPA displayed on screen! If you ever wish to restart, click 'Reset Table'. For any questions, click our interactive chat box at the bottom right of the Calculator Page or go to our 'More Information' page through the navigation bar. Your data is saved automatically when you click calculate. If you leave the website and come back, click 'Load Previous Data' in order to retrieve what you entered last time. Then you can add to the table or edit it (provided that you were signed in when you clicked Calculate, and when you clicked the load button.) There are also resources for help on how to improve your GPA :) This page can also be found in the navigation bar above. Happy Calculating!
